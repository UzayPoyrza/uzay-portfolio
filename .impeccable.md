## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Uzay as a candidate. They arrive scanning for signal: technical depth, taste, and personality. The site should reward both a quick skim (clear hierarchy, polished visuals) and a deeper look (interactive demos, thoughtful detail pages). The goal is to make them feel "this person builds things with care."

### Brand Personality
**Passionate, technical, warm.**
- Passionate: the work speaks for itself through live demos, real screenshots, and honest descriptions. Energy without hype.
- Technical: monospace labels, terminal aesthetics, and engineering detail signal craft. Not decorative complexity, but purposeful precision.
- Warm/playful: the terracotta palette, grain texture, serif headings, and loading screen humor ("Whatchamacalliting", "Simmering") keep it human and approachable. Never sterile.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Editorial meets dev-tool. Large Instrument Serif headings create magazine-like drama; Geist Sans body and monospace accents ground it in technical credibility.
- **Color:** Warm terracotta accent (#c45d3e) on near-black (#0c0c0c) with cream text (#e8e2d9). Light mode mirrors the warmth. No cold blues or generic tech gradients.
- **Texture:** Subtle grain overlay adds analog, physical quality. The site should feel crafted, not generated.
- **Motion:** Purposeful Framer Motion animations (fade-up on scroll, letter reveals, scale transitions). Always respect prefers-reduced-motion. Motion should feel natural, never flashy.
- **Theme:** Dark-first with a clean light mode toggle. Both themes share the same warm personality.

### Design Principles
1. **Show, don't tell.** Interactive demos, real screenshots, and terminal recordings over bullet-point feature lists. Let the work demonstrate competence.
2. **Warmth over polish.** Grain textures, playful copy, and a human color palette over cold, sterile perfection. The site should feel like a person, not a template.
3. **Precision in craft.** Consistent spacing (max-w-7xl, px-6, generous vertical rhythm), deliberate type hierarchy (serif headings, sans body, mono labels), and smooth transitions signal attention to detail.
4. **Accessible by default.** Semantic HTML, reduced-motion support, sufficient contrast, aria labels. Accessibility is not an afterthought.
5. **Content-first hierarchy.** Every visual choice serves readability and scanning. Large headings, muted supporting text, accent for interactive elements only.
